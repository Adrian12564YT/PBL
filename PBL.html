What is a Green Building?

A green building is one that refers to both a structure and the application of processes that are environmentally responsible and resource-efficient throughout a building's life-cycle: from panning to design, construction, operation, maintenance, renovation and demolition.


Why do we need Green Buildings?

When designing and constructing green buildings, the focus is on minimizing environmental impact and promoting sustainability throughout the lifecycle. There are several reasons to emphasize the importance of green buildings.


An example of a Green Building

  • The first Algae influenced building was built in Hamburg, Germany.
  • Wood log structures that store carbon, bricks made of mycelium and recycled textile bricks.
  • The Crystal, London, United Kingdom.
